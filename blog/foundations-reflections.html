Foundations Reflection

Oscar Harron July 21st, 2022

Five weeks have flown by and I have arrived at the end of Foundations. It is time to sit back and reflect.

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

Mindfulness and Awareness

A daily mindfulness practice has helped me to be more aware of my thoughts and ways of thinking. It has been interesting to get to know myself in this way.

Breaking Down Problems

I have a better understanding of how I problem solve. I am now able to break down problems into smaller chunks which become more solveable.

How to listen better

I have learnt to be a better listener. The listening and looping exercises showed me how distracted I can get. I am aware that I need to reduce distractions and focus on the person when I am listening. This way I will better understand the person.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

I think that having an awareness of your weaknesses, strengths, and values is critical to learning. If you know what you don't know then you become more open to learning.

Having a growth mindset is also a value that can boost your learning as it makes you open to growth and opportunity. We all have the capacity to learn and grow and become better.

What has suprised you the most about the core learning?

My biggest suprise was getting to understand myself better in the Identity, Values and Strengths activity.

It was an interesting activity and I got to write down thoughts that were floating around in my head somewhere but had never been expressed. I feel like I now have a better understanding of where my values and identity comes from.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

The most challenging aspects were writing down my learnings each week into a blog post. I found it difficult to distill a bunch of newly learnt material into easy to read, digestable blog posts.

I feel like I did an OK job ultimately but I feel like some of my writing was poor and hard to understand due to a lack of explanation.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a Web Development Bootcamp course?

I think it is because Dev Academy understands that being a well rounded individual is important to success in the tech industry and life in general.

It is not only about being technically proficent but having a good mindset, attitude and being able to understand and work well with others. More can be acheived by working with others compared to working alone.

Does the time you spent studing core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead?

I think the core material has been very useful in helping me become a more well rounded individual. It has helped me to get a better understanding about myself, how I think, how I learn and how I work with others.

I believe that these "soft skills" are very important in work and life in general as they enable cooperation with others which can often achieve a better result compared to working alone.

Knowing tech skills is also important but the tech industry can change very quickly and the skills that are important can also change.

This is why it is important to understand yourself so you can learn, reflect, adapt and grow. Once you understand yourself you tackle any challenge.

Oscar Harron
